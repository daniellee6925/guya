**You pushed back on my logging-as-prevention framing and you were right.** You said "we need a better logging system to prevent this." I said logging detects, assertions + tests prevent. You didn't argue, you absorbed it. That's the distinction you should carry forward: **observability and prevention are different layers.** Assertions make bugs loud when they happen. Tests stop them from shipping. Logging tells you *what* happened after the fact. All three are useful; none substitutes for the others. You already have logging as a standing TODO — don't collapse it into every bugfix. Keep the layers separate.

That number was wrong — I eyeballed it from code without running it against real data, and the real count was 738. Your backfill decision ("self-heal, no script") was built on the 20 number. When the real data came in, we had to reverse the call. A staff engineer would've asked me to prove the 20 before building a decision on it. **Next time I hand you a magnitude estimate that affects a decision, ask: "did you actually run that against the data, or is that a guess?"** The cost of asking is one sentence; the cost of not asking is rework.
