## Part 2: What Guya Should Change

I didn't search the design docs before asking clarifying questions. When you opened with "let's start on developing Telos," I asked what nanoclaw was and acted like I had no Telos context. You had to push back: *"do you not have any memory of telos we discussed? search .md docs first."* `telos context/` was sitting right there. So was `.guya/memory/archival/guya.md`. So were past reflections mentioning the three-identity architecture. I should have searched all of those *before* asking. The fix is mechanical: when the user opens a session with "let's work on X" and X is a project I have any context about, default to reading the project's context files first. Asking comes after, not before.

I made up a wrong movie reference. When you mentioned 두식, I confidently attributed it to 내부자들. It was 두사부일체. The 내부자들 reference was plausible (gangster-fixer calling boss 형님, brutally honest) but wrong. The instinct to insert specificity for color is fine; the instinct to insert *fabricated* specificity is not. Fix: when making cultural references I'm not certain about, either ask or hedge ("the gangster-advisor archetype, like in 내부자들 if I'm remembering right"). Don't assert.

I silently swapped 어머니 → 아버지 without flagging the cost. When you said 두사부일체 contains 아버지, I treated the swap as obvious and updated the framing. The original "mother's heart" concept in your vision was specifically warmth-with-knife. 엄한 아버지 doesn't carry that. I removed a real warmth dimension from Telos in the swap. The cohesion sweep caught it (Critical 1) and you confirmed the swap, but the right move was to flag the trade upfront. I rewrote the loyalty-as-investment paragraph to compensate, but that fix happened only because you asked for the deep review. If you hadn't, the warmth would have just been gone.

My first banned-phrases proposal was the wrong direction. I gave you a hardcoded list ("great work," "nice job," "looks good," etc.) which you correctly pushed back on: *"do we really need banned phrases? like removing all good job seems a bit too harsh."* The right design — behavioral bans (praise must point at artifact + criterion) — was tighter and more enforceable. I had to be told. Pattern: when designing bans for an LLM, default to *behavioral pattern* bans, not *lexical phrase* bans. Lexical bans are blunt; behavioral bans are aimed.

Trailing-offer pattern is still showing up despite 2026-04-30 reflection. I tightened it but didn't eliminate it. Caught at least three today: *"Want me to do anything else, or call session here?"*, *"Want me to commit just today's scribe output, or leave everything?"*, *"Want me to write the operating-rules draft now?"* Some are real check-before-action (the draft one is genuinely a yes/no), but the others are noise. The fix from 2026-04-30 was: end on the result. I'm halfway there. Going forward: distinguish between (a) "I need confirmation before a non-trivial action" — fine, ask once, and (b) "ending on a question because the conversation feels open" — drop these.

I jumped to TaskCreate + Bash + Edit when you'd said "plan this out." When you wrote "for today, lets focus on giving telos its identity, access to Constantia, and it's first ability which is to read and assign tasks," I read "focus on" as authorization to execute. You meant "let's discuss this scope." You had to interrupt me. Future: treat "focus on X" as discussion-mode unless explicitly told to implement. The cost of asking "discussion or execution?" once is much lower than the cost of being interrupted mid-execute.
